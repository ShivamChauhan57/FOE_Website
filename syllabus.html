Course Syllabus

I. Welcome!

Welcome to the Foundations of Engineering Lab course at the University of South Florida. This course aims to develop the Engineering mindset by engaging in a project-based learning module built on the framework of Design-thinking and Entrepreneurship. This course may utilize an in-person, asynchronous, and/or synchronous teaching model, so you may be required to login into the instructional platform (Canvas, MS Teams, Zoom, etc.) during class times. For all in-person meetings, it is encouraged that you wear masks when appropriate. Success in this course is dependent on your active participation in all assignments and engagement in group activities throughout the course.

III. University Course Description

Introduction to the profession of engineering. Exposure to the different disciplines of engineering incorporates examples of tools and techniques used in design and presentation. Laboratory exercises will include computer tools, engineering design, and presentation skills.

IV. Course Objectives

The objective of this course is to provide students in the College of Engineering with engineering design experience as a member of an interdisciplinary team. Students will design and fabricate a prototype that is relevant to the stakeholders and, while doing so, learn and practice essential skills useful in the engineering profession. Through this course, students will:
  1. Use software and hardware-based technologies as engineering tools to
    • design a prototype
    • fabricate a design,
    • test the design, and/or
    • optimize the design.
  2. Analyze the manufacturing process's opportunities or limitations when converting a design to a tangible and functional product that meets customer needs and has an entrepreneurial impact.
  3. Apply written and oral communication tools to articulate the design process's effects on a prototype, including input from shareholders and the individual steps of product design and development.
  4. Synthesize essential parts of engineering solutions to complex problems using the engineering design process and application of engineering technology.
  5. Apply engineering problem solving considering the roles reciprocity, teamwork, and reflection play in understanding the needs of a collaborator and/or fellow design team members through experiential learning.
  6. Demonstrate professional and ethical behaviors, interpersonal skills, and core competencies relevant for career preparedness in engineering or engineering-related field.

V. Student Learning Outcomes

Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:
  1. Create an original contribution within a specific discipline.
  2. Synthesize disparate or conflicting thoughts when evaluating questions or problems to form cohesive and collaborative solutions.
  3. Break down complex problems to examine, propose, and support potential solutions, even if those solutions deviate from acceptable, mainstream solutions.
  4. Analyze the opportunities or limitations of the manufacturing process when converting a design to a tangible and functional product that meets customer needs.
  5. Use CAD software, additive manufacturing technologies and other engineering tools to (1) design a prototype, (2) fabricate the design, (3) test the design, and/or (4) improve the design.
  6. Apply written and oral communication tools to articulate the effects of the design process on a final design including input from shareholders and the individual steps of product design and development.
  7. Demonstrate professional development, knowledge, and interpersonal skills useful for getting a job in an engineering or engineering-related field.
  8. Explore engineering problem solving considering the role reciprocity plays in understand both the transformational change of the community and their own personal edification.

Assessment:

Mastery of learning outcomes will be evaluated with individual and group assignments through Lab activities, oral and written communication, professional development activities, and project support. At least 50% of the assignments are based on individual assessments. All teamwork assignments require individual submissions with possible different grades earned within a group. No make-up labs or assignments are allowed. All late submissions will earn a score of 0, ten hours after the due date. “A penalty of 10% will be deducted from the total possible score per hour , or fraction thereof, late.” Most assignments will be assessed using Rubrics. Creative Thinking courses include a substantial writing component demonstrated in Written Communication 1-4. Approximate minimum word counts per submission are highlighted with the implementation of process writing.

Engineering Design-Thinking (38%):

Aspects of this course require critical and analytical thinking competencies given that students design a prototype, represent the design's characteristics using computer tools, fabricate/test a final product, and iterate, improve and optimize the design with a focus on educating elementary school-aged kids. Assignments in this category include CAD 2: Cosmetic Design, Iteration & Improving Designs, Fabrication, Design Review Presentation and Document, Design Optimization, and Project Testing

Engineering Lab Tutorials (15%):

The tutorials in this engineering lab course are guided worksheets in essential topic areas to aid in developing fundamental concepts necessary to complete projects. Assignments include Components 101, CAD 1, 3D printing, design process, programming, engineering disciplines, electronics & sensors, and basic statistical tools. Students are to bring their laptops and project kits to class during these lab activities.

Written Communication in Engineering (15%):

This course teaches students how to write discipline-specific written deliverables. Students write a professional memo and a technical report in the form of an executive summary, thereby providing students with two examples of standard communication methods in engineering. The writing assignments employ a process writing approach. Students draft a memo about their projects and receive feedback on their writing. Students then develop a plan for their revisions and execute that plan to revise their writing for final submission. The Word count of each memo is 500 words, while the executive summary document is 1500 words. Any tables and figures needed must be completed on MS Word or Excel with proper format.

Entrepreneurial Mindset (18%):

Students are to engage in entrepreneurship and engineering ethics & intellectual property modules. The ability to commercialize students' prototype and showcase it to stakeholders will be assessed.

Reflection and Professional Development (14%):

Industry and interpersonal competencies are integrated with the course through participation in reflection activities and professional development assignments. Students are required to enroll in USF's National Association of Colleges & Employers (NACE) career readiness badging program (CRBP) to document one professional competency and an ability to earn a digital career badge. Assignments include CRBP Learn it, CRBP Show it, and Project team evaluation survey.

Attendance:

Students are required to attend all scheduled classes or participate in an attendance assignment that demonstrates the attainment of the lesson covered.

X. Course Policies

Canvas & USF Email:

All students are provided with an official USF email address that is the primary means of communication between the students and the instructor outside of class. Students may set their USF accounts to forward emails to another address if preferred. All email correspondence with the instructor should have the course name/number written in the subject line and the student's U# in the email signature.

Attendance Policy:

Attendance is required and mandatory. Students must submit an online activity in MS Teams or other platforms directed by the Instructor/TAs during class times. Students who do not submit an online activity and/or do not attend a significant portion of the class is considered absent. Online activities that qualify for attendance may include submitting a forum post, a quiz, or an assignment. Failure to attend class reduces student's course attendance grade by 5%. Failure to attend 2 or more classes may result in an incomplete for the semester unless the instructor grants special permission.

Re-grading

Request for re-grading an assignment must be submitted to the instructor (or TA) within one week of the grade release. No re-grade will be entertained after this time.

Group Work Policy:

Students must take part in a group project. Once teams are formed, groups cannot be altered or switched. All members of a group may receive the same score depending on the assignment. Each group member must evaluate the team and provide the instructor with a suggested grade for other group members. The instructor then assigns a grade that is informed by those suggestions. The success of the group project is dependent on each member's contribution, be a team player.

Professionalism Policy:

Per university policy and classroom etiquette, mobile phones, iPods, etc., must be silenced during all classroom and lab lectures. Those not heeding this rule will be asked to leave the classroom/lab immediately to not disrupt the learning environment. Please arrive on time for all class meetings. Students who habitually disturb the class by talking, arriving late, etc., and have been warned may suffer a reduction in their final class grade.
"Netiquette" Guidelines
  1. Act professionally in the way you communicate. Treat your instructors and peers with respect, the same way as you would in a face-to-face environment. Respect other people’s ideas and be constructive when explaining your views about points you may not agree with.
  2. Be sensitive. Be respectful and sensitive when sharing your ideas and opinions. There will be people in your class with different linguistic backgrounds, political and religious beliefs, or other general differences.
  3. Proofread and check the spelling. Doing this before sending an email or posting a thread on a discussion board will allow you to make sure your message is clear and thoughtful. Avoid the use of all capital letters, it can be perceived as if you are shouting, and it is more difficult to read.
  4. Keep your communications focused and stay on topic. Complete your ideas before changing the subject. By keeping the message on focus, you allow the readers to easily get your idea or answers they are looking for.
  5. Be clear with your message. Avoid using humor or sarcasm. Since people can’t see your expressions or hear your tone of voice, meaning can be misinterpreted.
  6. Keep your emails and postings related to the course content. Any personal, course or confidential issues should be directly communicated to the instructor via email. The discussion boards are public spaces; therefore, any personal issues should not be posted there.

Copyrights:

Electronic recording of class lectures is not allowed without the instructor's permission. Class notes or files posted to the course website cannot be distributed or sold. Further, students shall not make any recording, including but not limited to audio and video recordings or photographs, during any class or meeting without the faculty member's permission.

Academic Integrity:

Students are expected to adhere to high standards of academic honesty and integrity, participate in class and group discussions and encourage the academic endeavors of their classmates. No form of academic dishonesty (cheating, plagiarism, etc.) will be tolerated. As commonly defined, plagiarism consists of passing off as one's own the ideas, words, writings, etc., which belong to another. In accordance with this definition, you are committing plagiarism if you copy the work of another person and turn it in as your own, even if you have the permission of that person. This includes copying material from books, reports, journals, pamphlets, handouts, other publications, web sites, etc., without giving appropriate credit for those ideas and/or without identifying material as quotations when taken directly from another source. The USF Policy on Academic Integrity specifies that students may not use websites that enable cheating, such as by uploading or downloading material for this purpose. This does apply specifically to CourseHero.com – any use of this website (including uploading materials) constitutes a violation of the academic integrity policy. Violation of these rules -- even unintentionally-- can result in disciplinary action including a grade penalty, up to and including an F or FF in the course, suspension, dismissal, and expulsion from USF. If you have any questions regarding plagiarism or other forms of scholastic dishonesty, please consult the relevant sections of the USF student catalogs, and/or ask the instructor. Students are strongly encouraged to complete USF's online plagiarism tutorial, which may be found at: http://www.cte.usf.edu/plagiarism/plag.html

Turnitin.com

In this course, turnitin.com will be utilized. Accordingly, you will be expected to submit all written assignments in electronic format. After the assignment is processed, as instructor will receive a report from turnitin.com that states if and how another author’s work was used in the assignment. Disruption to Academic Progress:

Disruptive students in the academic setting hinder the educational process. Disruption of the academic process (USF Regulation 3.025) is defined as the act, words, or general conduct of a student in a classroom or other academic environment which in the reasonable estimation of the instructor: (a) directs attention away from the academic matters at hand, such as noisy distractions, persistent, disrespectful or abusive interruption of lecture, exam, academic discussion, or general University operations, or (b) presents a danger to the health, safety, or well-being of self or other persons.
